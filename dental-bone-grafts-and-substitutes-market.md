<p><p>The dental bone grafts and substitutes market consists of several key types: Demineralized Bone Matrix (DBM), which is derived from human bone and retains growth factors to promote healing; Xenografts, sourced from animal bones, typically bovine, providing structural support while minimizing disease transmission risks; and Synthetic grafts, made from biocompatible materials like calcium phosphate or hydroxyapatite, offering customization and stability. These options aim to restore bone volume and facilitate dental implant success, each catering to varying clinical needs.</p></p>
