<p><p>The High Integrity Pressure Protection System (HIPPS) market consists of two primary types: electronic and mechanical systems. Electronic HIPPS utilize advanced sensors, control systems, and software to continuously monitor pressure levels and trigger automatic shutdowns when necessary, ensuring enhanced reliability and response times. In contrast, mechanical HIPPS rely on traditional physical components like pressure relief valves and actuators to manage pressure risks. Both systems aim to enhance safety in industrial applications by preventing over-pressurization and protecting critical assets.</p></p>
