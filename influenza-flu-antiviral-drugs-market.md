<p><ul><li>Penetration and Dehulling Inhibitors</li><li>DNA Polymerase Inhibitors</li><li>Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors</li><li>Protein Inhibitors</li><li>Neuraminidase Inhibitors</li><li>Broad-spectrum Antiviral Drugs</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The influenza antiviral drugs market comprises several types targeting different viral mechanisms. Penetration and dehulling inhibitors prevent viral entry into host cells, while DNA polymerase inhibitors block the replication process of viral DNA. Reverse transcriptase inhibitors disrupt the conversion of viral RNA into DNA. Protein inhibitors target specific viral proteins essential for reproduction, and neuraminidase inhibitors hinder viral release from infected cells. Broad-spectrum antiviral drugs offer a versatile approach by targeting various viruses, enhancing treatment options for influenza and other viral infections.</p></p>
