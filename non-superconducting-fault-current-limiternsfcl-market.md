<p><p>Non-superconducting Fault Current Limiters (NSFCL) are devices designed to limit fault currents in electrical systems, enhancing safety and reliability. They are primarily classified into two market types: saturable core and solid-state. Saturable core NSFCL utilizes magnetic materials that switch properties during high fault currents, effectively reducing the current flow. In contrast, solid-state NSFCL employs semiconductor technology to control and limit fault currents swiftly and efficiently, offering faster response times and enhanced performance in modern electrical grids.</p></p>
