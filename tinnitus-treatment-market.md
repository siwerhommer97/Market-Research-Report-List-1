<p><ul><li>Subjective Tinnitus</li><li>Objective Tinnitus</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Tinnitus Treatment Market encompasses two main applications: subjective and objective tinnitus. Subjective tinnitus, the more common form, involves sounds perceived only by the patient and is often tied to underlying conditions like hearing loss or exposure to loud noise. Treatments typically focus on managing symptoms through therapies, sound masking, or counseling. Objective tinnitus, though rare, involves sounds detectable by others, often linked to physical issues such as blood vessel disorders. Treatment may involve addressing the underlying medical conditions to alleviate symptoms.</p></p>
