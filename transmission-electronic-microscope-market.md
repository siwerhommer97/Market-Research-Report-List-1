<p><ul><li>Conventional TEM</li><li>Low-Voltage Electron Microscope</li><li>Cryo-microscopy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Transmission Electron Microscope (TEM) market comprises several key types. Conventional TEM utilizes high-energy electrons for imaging, providing detailed structural information at the atomic level but requiring extensive sample preparation. Low-Voltage Electron Microscopes operate at lower voltages, minimizing damage to sensitive samples while still offering high-resolution imaging, ideal for biological specimens. Cryo-microscopy preserves samples at cryogenic temperatures, allowing for the observation of biological structures in their natural state without traditional staining or preparation, making it crucial for life sciences research.</p></p>
